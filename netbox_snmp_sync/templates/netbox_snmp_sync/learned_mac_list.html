What are Learned MACs?

During each SNMP sync, the engine reads the bridge forwarding table (dot1dTpFdbTable / Q-BRIDGE) from every managed switch. Each entry records a MAC address that the switch has seen arrive on a particular port.

These entries are stored here so you can track what end-devices are connected to your network without requiring agents or NetBox devices for every endpoint.

Only Learned-type entries are kept — the switch's own MAC addresses (Self / Mgmt) are filtered out.

Status Values
New First time this MAC has been seen — not yet confirmed active.
Active Seen in the most recent sync run.
Stale Present in a previous sync but absent from recent runs. Use Mark Stale to bulk-flag entries not seen in 48 hours.
Promoted Manually converted into a full NetBox device record via the Promote action.
Promoting a MAC

If a MAC address belongs to a device you want to manage in NetBox, click the Promote button () in the list or on the detail page.

This creates a new NetBox Device record, assigns it a site, role, and device type, and links the MAC address to its interface. The Learned MAC entry is then marked Promoted and won't be promoted again.

Only Active or New entries with a known source interface are eligible for promotion.
